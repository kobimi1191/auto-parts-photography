  • Check focus on monitor (zoom 10x to verify)
  • Take PKG shot (must be able to clearly read part numbers
  • Clean part (remove dust, fingerprints, stickers)
  • Take Shot 1 - Front View (0¬∞) DO NOT SHOOT ABOVE THE PART
  • Rotate part to show the first three sides
  • Take Shot 2 - Isometric perspective angle #1
  • Rotate and/or flip part to show the opposite three sides.
  • Take Shot 3 - Isometric perspective angle #2
  • Take Shot 4 (if required) - Alternate angle shot of any connections, plugs, etc. missed in other shots
  • Review ALL shots on monitor before removing part
  • If any shot is blurry or bad - RESHOOT NOW
  • Move to next part

SPECIAL SITUATIONS

Reflective Parts & Large Parts

‚ö†Ô∏è REFLECTIVE PARTS (Chrome, Polished Metal)

Common Reflective Parts:

  • Chrome bumpers
  • Polished alternators
  • Exhaust tips (stainless)
  • Pulleys and brackets
  • Valve covers (chrome)
  • Any shiny metal surface

What To Do:

  • Use diffusion panels on lights
  • Move lights farther away (48"+)
  • Check monitor for hotspots
  • Use dulling spray if needed
  • Angle lights at 90¬∞ not 45¬∞
  • ASK FOR HELP if unsure
üì¶ LARGE PARTS - USE STATION C

Large Parts Include:

  • Front/rear bumpers
  • Hoods and trunk lids
  • Transmissions
  • Engine blocks
  • Full exhaust systems
  • Large body panels

Station C Features:

  • Floor sweep backdrop (9ft wide)
  • Raised camera position
  • Lights positioned farther back
  • More workspace for positioning
  • Use cart/dolly for heavy parts
üõë STOP AND GET HELP IF...
  • You've taken 3+ shots and none look good

  • You're unsure if part is reflective
  • Part is damaged and you notice after shooting
