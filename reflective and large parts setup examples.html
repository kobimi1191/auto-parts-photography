Anti-Reflection Setup

  • Diffusion tent surrounds part
  • Polarizing filter on camera lens
  • Polarizing film on lights (optional)
  • Lights at 90° angle (not 45°)
  • Move lights farther away (48"+)
  • Use dulling spray if needed

Common Reflective Parts

  • Chrome bumpers
  • Polished alternators
  • Stainless exhaust tips
  • Shiny pulleys
  • Metal fuel tanks
  • Valve covers

⚠️ Critical for Reflective Parts

If you see hotspots or reflections in the monitor, STOP and adjust lighting before continuing. Reflective parts cannot be fixed in post-processing.

Large Parts Adjustments

  • Use 9ft seamless paper (wall to floor)
  • Raise camera height or use wider lens
  • Move lights farther back (60"+)
  • May need overhead light for depth
  • Part sits on floor portion of sweep
  • Use cart/dolly for heavy parts

Large Part Examples

  • Front/rear bumpers
  • Hoods and trunk lids
  • Transmissions
  • Engine blocks
  • Exhaust systems
  • Full dashboards

💡 Pro Tip

For very large parts, consider a dedicated Station C with permanent floor sweep setup. Saves setup time and ensures consistency.

Universal Rules (All Setups)

✓ Always Check Monitor

✓ Lock Settings

Never change camera settings mid-shoot
